The Role

What is a
Chief Agent Officer?

As AI agents proliferate across every business function, organisations need a dedicated executive to own the strategy, governance, and outcomes.

🧠

Not just AI strategy

The CAO goes beyond the CAIO. They don't just advise on AI — they run the agents, own the infrastructure, and are accountable for outcomes delivered by autonomous systems.

⚙️

Operational, not advisory

Where the CTO builds the systems and the CDO manages the data, the CAO operates the agents that sit on top — orchestrating workflows, managing risk, and driving ROI.

🛡️

Governance owner

Autonomous agents make decisions. Someone must be accountable. The CAO owns the guardrails, audit trails, escalation paths, and ethical frameworks that keep agents aligned.

🚀

Competitive differentiator

The companies that scale agents fastest will win. The CAO is the executive function that turns ad-hoc AI experiments into a coordinated fleet of business-critical agents.

Mandate

The CAO's six
core responsibilities

Owning AI agents is a full-time executive job. Here's what it looks like in practice.

01

Agent Strategy & Portfolio

Define which agents get built, bought, or banned. Prioritise the agent portfolio against business outcomes — not technology novelty.

02

Governance & Risk

Set the rules agents operate under. Own the risk frameworks, compliance controls, and escalation protocols that keep autonomous systems safe.

03

Infrastructure & Tooling

Partner with engineering to select agent runtimes, observability stacks, memory systems, and the orchestration layers that agents run on.

04

Cross-Functional Deployment

Drive agent adoption across sales, ops, finance, HR, and security — removing the friction that keeps agents stuck in proof-of-concept.

05

Performance & ROI

Define agent KPIs, measure the value delivered, and report outcomes to the board. The CAO makes the business case visible and defensible.

06

Talent & Culture

Build the team that builds the agents. Champion AI-native culture, upskill existing teams, and attract the specialists who know how agents actually work.
